The Bridge Builder

Posted November 7, 2021  |  Dedicated to our Past Masters

By Will Allen Dromgoole

An old man going a lone highway,
Came, at the evening cold and gray,
To a chasm vast and deep and wide,
Through which was flowing a sullen tide【249558912708886†L15-L17】. The old man crossed in the twilight dim and then turned to build a bridge for those who followed him. When questioned, he replied that a fair‑haired youth would soon cross the same chasm, and his bridge would make the passage safe【249558912708886†L18-L28】.

This touching poem reminds us that the Past Masters of our lodge built a strong foundation for future generations.
